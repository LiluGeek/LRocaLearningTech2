LEARNING TECHNOLOGIES

MULTIPLE INTELLIGENCES

A STUDY ON MOOC DROPOUT RATES

AND

MULTIPLE INTELLIGENCES

 

ABSTRACT

 

This paper explores the partnership between how people learn, and Learning Technologies (LT).  It discusses discoveries about learning styles, Gardner’s multiple intelligences, the difference between learning styles and multiple intelligences, and how LT can be utilized to produce effective technology-based instruction that is theoretically sound.  This paper offers insights on how technology-based instruction can enhance learning by developing instructional designs to target the unique manner students learn.

 

 Read this professional paper In Sribd.com

 

 

ABSTRACT

 

Since the inception of massive open online courses (MOOCs), this platform for learning has been characterized as having high enrollment rates with high dropout rates.  Researchers are studying this phenomenon at length, but there is no unifying consensus about underlying factors for high dropout rates in MOOCs.  This mixed method research study aims to discover whether the wide variety of reasons found for dropout rates are connected to instructional design as it pertains to learners with different types of intelligences. This research study will have a purposive sampling design with a control group and a treatment group of learners to determine whether a redesigned MOOC with instructional techniques for learners with different types of intelligences can improve retention rates and reduce dropout rates. Also, the data may provide the unifying factor for the wide variety of reasons found for high dropout rates in this type of massive form of online education.

    A SHORT STORY

     

    GENTLE GIANTS

     

    The gentle giants in the highlands of Peru inspire a dance of all the senses.  Their sweet aroma, like a dream one never wants to wake up from, drifts through the invisible embrace of the highland winds.  Its aromatic elixir, soft like the giants themselves, penetrates the core of one’s being because the breeze never fails to deliver the forest’s perfume.  With each inhalation, the aroma of the giants takes one’s breath away.  High above sea level, 6,000 meters above the rest of the world, the view beneath the mountains, like a mantle of the finest silk, the clouds and the morning midst embrace these quiet giants; gentle, tall, dark-turquoise giants of the highlands of Peru, they stand tall and proud imposing their presence and yet submissive to the gaze of a young girl.

     

    As the child becomes perplexed and awes at their magical presence, the forests speak their quiet lyrics.  Every leaf and every branch sway and turn in a harmony of shades of green.  Dark green, its turquoise extremities show off their leaves like fine jewels dancing in the wind, and the branches quiver to speak their secret song.  They whisper in a hushed voice that hums their gentle song to quiet the most disturbed of souls.  Small and narrow dirt roads sway around the giants’ slender bodies.  Dust and dirt levitate with the wind of man’s presence. Mankind disturbs the forest’s perfect dance of sight, smell and touch while steps hit the ground and eyes look up at the gentle giants’ silhouette.  It is the breeze!  That is the culprit!  The guilty one that coax these giants to reveal their secrets. They sing and reveal their secret songs calling for mankind to listen, to be quiet, to allow them to speak, to allow them to live.  The gentle, gorgeous, majestic Eucalyptus forests of the highlands of Peru. Will mankind listen to their song, or will mankind silence them forever.

GAME-BASED LEARNING

 

ABSTRACT

 

This professional paper explores the subject of game-based learning in education and the reasons instructors resist its use.  This paper gives a perspective on how much 21st-century students have changed from years past, the reason game-based learning is relevant in today’s society, the advent of games in the classroom, arguments for and against games in instruction, educational theories that support its use, games as a form of problem-based learning, methods of instruction utilizing games, teachers that embrace versus reject  games in curricula, and how it can be  adopted in the field of education in the public and private sector.
